The Einstein--Hilbert action \checkthis{in vacuum} is \comment{check Planck mass def.}
\begin{equation}
    S\ped{EH} = \shalf \Planck{M}^2 \int \! \diff[4]x \,  \mathcal{R}, 
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{R} = g\upind{\mu\nu}\mathcal{R}\loind{\mu\nu}$. By varying $S\ped{EH}$ with respect to $g\loind{\mu\nu}$ one obtains the equation of motion
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{G}\loind{\mu\nu} \equiv \mathcal{R}\loind{\mu\nu} + \frac{1}{2} g\loind{\mu\nu} \mathcal{R} = 0.
\end{equation}
Thus, we interpret GR \emph{classical} field theory where the tensor field $g\loind{\mu\nu}$ is the gravitational field, \cringe{with the particle \grammar[z?]{realisation} named ``graviton''}.
